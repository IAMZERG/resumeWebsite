Hello, my name is Wes.

I'm a pretty dull person. Black and white are among my favorite colors, as you might guess... I am well-educated. I have Bachelor of Science in Biology. I began my education in Computer Engineering and Computer Science, but took some time off, changed my mind, and became a biology major over a year into the program.

What makes Wes tick?

My experience in programming came both from high school and from college. Unfortunately, from that experience, I was led to believe that a career in programming would necessarily be a boring one. Code Louisville and their use of the Treehouse curriculum has proven my notions wrong. Learning to write HTML, use CSS, and write JavaScript has been an enjoyable, pain-free experience so far. Creating webpages using these elements seems a lot less daunting than I thought it would be.

I haven't been at this very long, but my skills have grown quite a bit since I first started.

Go on a brief journey with me to see who I am and how I have changed.

Experience

My web design experience comes through a program called Code Louisville put on by Kentuckiana Works. View their website here I have also put some of my work on Github here.

Taught myself Python during my free time. I do not yet have much to show for it, however. I was working on an application for playtesting Magic: the Gathering decks and manipulating statistics from the games, but have mostly abandoned it for now. I may revisit it later.

Education

Code Louisville: A class designed to empower the Louisville community and train more coders/web designers here in Louisville.

Treehouse is a website devoted to web design and development education. The Louisville Free Public Library provides free access to the materials. The site covers everything from the artsy side of things like typography to the technical (e.g. PHP).

Started my college career in the JB Speed School of Engineering seeking a degree in Computer Engineering and Computer Science, but calculus gave me a lot of trouble.

My Bachelor of Science in Biology with a Genetics/Subcellular focus was completed with a 2.9 GPA. The time I spent pursuing this degree was worth it because of how much I enjoyed it, but work for non-medical Biology students is sparse in Louisville. I’d prefer to stay here in Louisville, but am open to moving if the right opportunity presents itself.

Graduated from Eastern High School. The school is a technology magnet (computers and such). Spent some time learning basic C++ and computer repair skills.

Hobbies

I’m a gamer, and that consumes more of my time than it probably should. It affects how I talk, the types of people I tend to spend time with, and it has helped to shape my passions and goals in life. I’ve even got a complete list of games that are near and dear to me if you’re curious.

It’s a little strange having learning as a hobby, but it is indeed one of mine. I often get really excited and obsessive when I learn something new. However, this can make learning new skills pretty difficult at times. For example, during my time pursuing my Biology degree, I became obsessed with quorum sensing systems and devoted much of the time that should have been spent studying other things to studying that.

I DO enjoy programming as a hobby, and it has been something that has filled more of my time more recently than it ever did in the past.

I participate in a casual league at Something to Do on Monday nights. In spite of how long I've been in the game, I'm still pretty bad at it.

Goals

I also want to do some freelance work, particularly on things that can grow and stretch me. I like learning, particularly things that are practical. Shoot me a message, tell me what you’re working on, and let’s get together.
